Friends

I am fortunate.

I don’t know if he wants to be named, but my housemate made gradpay.org where people can learn and share what institutions provide the best support for their grad students.

Zach Babb started an organization at my school to build cool stuff, designed a mobile ticketing app for my town’s transit system, and has helped me understand completely every physics question I’ve asked of him. He is a natural teacher and profoundly generous.

Annelyse Gelman is one of the most inspiring people I know: artist, scientist, and deeply engaged human. I love her photography, music, poetry, and self.

Mark Hintz works at Periscopic. When I saw the visualization of U.S. gun murders he helped to make, I was awed and devastated.

Rose Lewis is an artist in many media: photography and writing, ink, paint, music, and cats. She loves punk, tea, flowers, and Russian literature/history/language/soup. Also cats. This is our cat (joint custody).

Jen Polachek is eclectic and tough, and her interests -- from yoga to photography to food to art -- converge on a perspective on people and their relationships to their bodies that I find deeply humbling, and that guides my own self-care. Though it no longer quite accords with how she wants to write, she had for a while a beautiful food blog I still visit for ideas.

Arianna Rebolini is my favorite non-fiction writer. She transformed how I think about television as a medium (if ever she writes a piece on this I will link it here) and taught me the value of accepting people as they are. You should read her pieces on The Hairpin for a brief idea of how wise and funny she is.

Jeannie Yoon is a linguistics major and a fierce woman. Her facility with language has such fine resolution her prose is lyrical and her poetry is cogent. She makes delicious kimchi.
