The Thesis

If you are here because you’d like to participate, click here!

If you’re still not sure, here’s the pitch.

This is a relatively brief EEG experiment (roughly 60 minutes’ prep time, and roughly 20 minutes’ task time) with two purposes: one is as a straightforward EEG experiment looking for a reliable ERP component, i.e. a signal correlated to a cognitive event, corresponding to making errors in a simple computer task. Not only do I receive valuable training in a fairly new and burgeoning experimental procedure, but I also learn more about being a good scientist in general.

The second purpose pertains to the first half of my experiment, which is a direct replication of a published research finding. This is part of the Open Science Collaboration, which I’ve linked to below. An international group of scientists is cooperating as we speak to explore the extent to which published research is replicable, and also to figure out new ways to improve replicability, because it is a foundational principle of science.

EEG is an exciting way of examining neurophysiological correlates of cognition and even consciousness, but much work needs to be done to standardize it and otherwise make data available to people. I believe professional science as it stands today is in need of significant reform, but I’m hopeful this reform is possible and look forward to participating in realizing a more open, transparent, collaborative future for science and information in general.
